Welcome!

Hi there. NBP-1 is a test flight we are performing with the goal of testing our telemetry systems. We welcome the community to follow along in our efforts, but ask everyone to keep in mind that this is a test flight. Have fun and stay safe!

- The NBP-1 Team

What is this project?

This flight (NBP-1) is the first of hopefully many high-altitude balloon flights which will be launched by the Northeast Ohio Experimenters Club.

The flight is meant to test our telemetry communication systems such as arduino-rtty, mapview, mapview's frontend , and more. We will also be recording HD video from the payload and running a production mapview instance for the general public to use in tracking the balloon online.

Who is behind it?

We are the Northeast Ohio Experimenters Club , a new amateur radio and electronics club started in the Fall of 2014. NOEXC is comprised of members residing all throughout the northeast Ohio area with member coverage extending as far noth as Cleveland, as far east as Youngstown, and as far west as Akron/Canton. Please visit our wiki for more information.

Stay in the know.

We will (may?) be live-streaming the launch of NBP-1. Once it is up in the air, you can track it in a number of ways. First, you can, of course, turn on a radio and decode the RTTY telemetry data using a computer or smartphone. If that's not an option, however, you can use our production "mapview" system which allows you to track the balloon in your browser from anywhere with an internet connection, in realtime. More information will appear here before the launch.
