Welcome!

Welcome to the NOEXC Balloon Project information landing site. NBP-3 will launch on June 27th during the ARRL Field Day event. In addition to beaconing RTTY telemetry tracking-data, our payload will also include a 2-meter FM voice repeater which will enable all licensed amateur radio operators with access to 2-meters in the region to communicate amongst each other via the balloon. We welcome and encourage the community to track our efforts. We will publish more information as it is available. Please register on our Wiki and our mailing list to track the latest developments within the project.

- The NBP-3 Team

What is this project?

This flight (NBP-3) is a high-altitude balloon flight which will be launched by the Northeast Ohio Experimenters Club .

The goal of this flight is to enable amateur radio operators in the region of Ohio and possibly surrounding states to communicate with each other through the balloon.

Who is behind it?

We are the Northeast Ohio Experimenters Club , a new amateur radio and electronics club started in the Fall of 2014. NOEXC is comprised of members residing all throughout the northeast Ohio area with member coverage extending as far north as Cleveland, as far east as Youngstown, and as far west as Akron/Canton. Please visit our wiki for more information.

Stay in the know.

We will (may?) be live-streaming the launch of NBP-3. Once it is up in the air, you can track it in a number of ways. First, you can, of course, turn on a radio and decode the RTTY telemetry data using a computer or smartphone. If that's not an option, however, you can use our production "mapview" system which allows you to track the balloon in your browser from anywhere with an internet connection, in realtime. More information will appear here before the launch.
